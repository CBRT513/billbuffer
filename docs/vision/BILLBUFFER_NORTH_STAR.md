# BillBuffer North Star

> **Read the [Constitution](./BILLBUFFER_CONSTITUTION.md) first — it is the supreme
> authority and wins any conflict.** The Constitution says what BillBuffer _must_ and
> _must not_ do; the [Philosophy](./BILLBUFFER_PHILOSOPHY.md) says what we believe. This
> page is the compass for the space they leave open: **when a product decision is
> genuinely up to you, this is what to optimize for.**

## Optimize for one thing

**A person's confidence in a single moment — _"Can I safely spend this right now?"_**

Every product decision should make that moment **clearer, calmer, and quicker to
trust.** If it doesn't, reconsider it.

We are not optimizing for more features, more engagement, or completeness for its own
sake. We optimize for how surely — and how calmly — someone can answer that one
question and get on with their day.

## The tie-breaker

The Constitution decides what is allowed. When more than one allowed option remains,
choose the one that lets a person:

1. **Reach the answer with less effort** — fewer steps, less to enter, less to think about.
2. **Trust the answer more** — obvious where the number comes from, no hidden assumptions, no false certainty.
3. **Feel calmer, not more anxious** — never shame, never scare, never pressure.

An option that adds capability but loses on any of these is the wrong option.

## When these pull apart

- **Clarity over completeness.** A simpler answer someone trusts beats a fuller one they don't.
- **Trust over speed.** Honestly slower beats confidently wrong.
- **Calm over cleverness.** If a smarter-looking answer raises anxiety, it isn't smarter.

## Scope

This compass is for **product** choices only. It never overrides the Constitution, and
it says nothing about _how_ the app is built — technical limits and engineering
trade-offs live in the
[Architecture Guardrails](../architecture/ARCHITECTURE_GUARDRAILS.md) and the
engineering docs, not here.
