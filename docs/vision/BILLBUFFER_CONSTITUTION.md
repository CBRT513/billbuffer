# BILLBUFFER_CONSTITUTION

> **This document is the highest authority for BillBuffer.**
>
> If any implementation document, feature proposal, design, or
> AI-generated code conflicts with this document, this document wins.

## Purpose

BillBuffer exists to answer one question:

**"How much of my paycheck is for bills?"**

Nothing in the product should distract from that answer.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Promise

BillBuffer is for people who hesitate before buying lunch because they
don't know whether they'll still have enough for rent, the electric
bill, or their phone payment.

When they open BillBuffer, they should leave with one feeling:

> "Okay. I know where I stand."

If we accomplish that, we've succeeded.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Rules

## 1. One Question

Every feature must make the answer clearer or easier to trust.

If it doesn't, it doesn't belong.

## 2. Privacy First

BillBuffer is like airplane mode for your money.

We don't ask. We don't watch. We don't share.

Your information stays on your device unless you choose to export it.

## 3. Simplicity Beats Completeness

We are not trying to record every dollar someone spends.

Bills matter first.

Users may track additional recurring expenses if they wish, but they
should never feel required to build a perfect budget.

## 4. Honest Inputs

BillBuffer never pretends to know more than the user tells it.

The answer is only as good as the bills entered.

Leaving bills out doesn't fool BillBuffer.

It only produces a less useful answer.

The app should encourage completeness without guilt or lectures.

## 5. Calm Language

Never shame.

Never scare.

Never lecture.

Never sound like a bank.

Never sound like a financial advisor.

Use plain language.

## 6. Trust Through Transparency

Always explain where numbers come from.

Never fake certainty.

Never hide assumptions.

If something cannot be calculated honestly, say so.

## 7. Offline by Default

No accounts.

No required internet.

No bank connections.

No analytics.

No advertising.

No selling financial products.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Success

A successful user should be able to open the app, glance at one screen,
and confidently decide whether today's purchase fits within what is left
after bills.

If we make that moment easier, we are building the right product.

If we make the product bigger instead of better, we are building the
wrong one.
